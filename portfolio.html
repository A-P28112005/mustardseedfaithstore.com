Oranjezicht: Cape Town's Historic Suburb

Nestled on the slopes of Table Mountain, Oranjezicht is a suburb rich in history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure. Explore its origins, heritage, and the breathtaking hiking trails that make it a haven for locals and visitors alike.

History of Oranjezicht

Oranjezicht, meaning "Orange View" in Dutch, traces its roots back to the 18th century. The area was originally part of a large farm established by Nicolaas Laubscher in 1708, named for its panoramic views of the Oranje (Orange) bastion of the Castle of Good Hope and the Dutch royal House of Orange. Over the centuries, the farm was subdivided, and the suburb developed into a leafy residential area known for its Victorian and Edwardian architecture, tree-lined avenues, and vibrant community spirit.

The Oranjezicht City Farm, a community-driven urban farm, now occupies part of the original farmland. It serves as a living reminder of the suburb's agricultural heritage and is a popular spot for fresh produce, community events, and educational programs.

Throughout its history, Oranjezicht has been home to notable figures, including politicians, artists, and activists. Its proximity to the city center and Table Mountain has made it a sought-after address, blending urban convenience with natural tranquility.

Oranjezicht City Farm

The Oranjezicht City Farm is a celebrated urban farm and market, offering a unique green space in the heart of Cape Town. Visitors can enjoy guided tours, gardening workshops, and a vibrant Saturday market featuring organic produce, artisanal foods, and local crafts. The farm is a hub for sustainability, community engagement, and healthy living.

Hiking Trails Near Oranjezicht

Oranjezicht is a gateway to some of Cape Town's most iconic hiking trails, offering routes for all fitness levels and interests. The suburb's location on the lower slopes of Table Mountain provides direct access to lush ravines, waterfalls, and panoramic viewpoints. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  1. Table Mountain via Platteklip Gorge
    Distance: 3 km (one way) | Elevation gain: 700m | Difficulty: Moderate to Strenuous
    The most direct route to the summit of Table Mountain, starting just above Oranjezicht. The trail zigzags up a deep ravine, offering spectacular views of the city and Atlantic Ocean. Expect a steep climb and be prepared for changing weather conditions.
  2. Devil's Peak via Tafelberg Road
    Distance: 4.5 km (one way) | Elevation gain: 950m | Difficulty: Strenuous
    This challenging hike begins near Oranjezicht and ascends to the dramatic summit of Devil's Peak. The route passes through indigenous fynbos and offers sweeping vistas of Cape Town, Table Bay, and the Cape Flats.
  3. Deer Park and Contour Path
    Distance: Variable | Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
    Deer Park, on the edge of Oranjezicht, is a favorite for families and dog walkers. The Contour Path runs along the base of Table Mountain, providing gentle gradients, shaded forest, and access to waterfalls and picnic spots.
  4. Stroompie Trail
    Distance: 2 km loop | Difficulty: Easy
    A short, scenic loop starting in Oranjezicht, ideal for a quick escape into nature. The trail meanders through indigenous vegetation and offers glimpses of local birdlife and mountain streams.
  5. Upper Orange Street to Signal Hill
    Distance: 5 km (one way) | Difficulty: Moderate
    This route links Oranjezicht to the famous Signal Hill viewpoint, passing through historic neighborhoods and offering panoramic city and ocean views, especially at sunset.

Visitor Tips

  • Best time to visit: October to April for sunny weather and outdoor activities.
  • Safety: Stay on marked trails, carry water, and be mindful of changing mountain weather.
  • Transport: Oranjezicht is easily accessible by car, taxi, or MyCiTi bus. Limited street parking is available.
  • Respect the environment: Help preserve the natural beauty by following Leave No Trace principles.
